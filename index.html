Arthur Morgan... a man searching for peace in a world of bullets, betrayal, and blood — a peace he would never find.

Born into a ruthless world that refused to cradle a motherless child. A thief for a father, and a heart hardened by Dutch, who raised him to survive, not to feel.

Arthur believed himself to be a bad man — the one who did the gang’s dirty work without flinching. But behind those cold eyes lay a buried heart, one that still beat for love, forgiveness, and redemption.

Tuberculosis didn’t just destroy his body — it awakened his soul. Every cough, every sleepless night, was a whisper pulling him away from the darkness. Perhaps redemption was still within reach.

And he tried... through pain, through blood, with every last breath. In the end, Arthur died — not as a thief, but as a man. A man whose silence echoed forever in the hearts of millions.

آرتور خودش را یک مرد بد می‌دانست. کسی که کارهای کثیف باند را بی‌هیچ تردیدی انجام می‌داد. اما پشت آن نگاه سرد و بی‌احساس، قلبی دفن‌شده بود؛ قلبی که هنوز برای عشق، برای دوست‌داشتن، برای بخشیدن می‌تپید.
